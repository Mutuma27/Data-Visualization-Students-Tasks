User Interaction Design Documentation

Dashboard Architecture

Built as a single-page web dashboard using Chart.js 4.4.1 for all visualisations. The system is fully client-side with no backend dependency, enabling offline use and rapid deployment.

All 481 survey records are embedded inline. Filter operations run in real time using JavaScript array methods, recomputing all derived statistics on each interaction.

Interactive Controls

  • Gender slicer isolates Male or Female subsets across all charts and KPIs simultaneously.
  • Occupation slicer segments by University Student, School Student, Salaried Worker, or Retired.
  • Relationship slicer filters by Single, In a relationship, Married, or Divorced.
  • Metric toggles add or remove any of 6 mental health indicators from the trend line chart.
  • Reset control restores all filters to the full 481-respondent baseline in one click.

KPI Tracking and Performance Metrics

  • Total respondents in current filter scope
  • Heavy usage rate (more than 5h/day) as a percentage
  • Purposeless usage rate (scores 4 to 5 on Likert scale)
  • Average depression score across usage brackets
  • Average sleep disruption score across usage brackets
  • Most adopted platform in current filter scope

All KPIs recalculate live on every filter change.

User-Centred Design Principles

  • Every chart and KPI card carries a contextual tooltip (i icon) explaining what the metric means, how to read it, and what the data shows.
  • Colour coding is consistent: blue for neutral data, red for risk indicators, green for adoption metrics.
  • Screen-reader accessible via aria-label attributes on all canvas elements and an sr-only heading.
  • Responsive grid layouts reflow at 960px, 660px, and 540px breakpoints for tablet and mobile support.
  • Hover states on every interactive element provide immediate affordance feedback.
Decision-Support Use Cases
Use Case 1
University Student Mental Health Review
A student welfare officer wants to understand which mental health indicators are most elevated among university students. They apply the Occupation filter to isolate this group and toggle all 6 metrics on the trend chart to identify distraction and depression as the highest-scoring concerns at heavy usage levels.
Filter: Occupation = University Student
Use Case 2
Gender-Based Platform Risk Assessment
A public health researcher needs to compare how platform usage correlates with social comparison and validation-seeking across genders. They toggle between Male and Female filters while observing the gender bar chart and platform adoption bars to identify whether Instagram usage aligns with elevated comparison scores in female respondents.
Filter: Gender = Female, then Male
Use Case 3
Salaried Worker Productivity Impact
An HR analyst wants to assess whether heavy social media use among employed adults correlates with distraction and sleep disruption. Using the Occupation and Relationship filters together, they isolate Salaried Workers and examine distraction and sleep trends in the line chart, supporting a case for digital wellness policies.
Filter: Occupation = Salaried Worker
Use Case 4
Teenage Screen Time Intervention Planning
A school counsellor needs data to justify limiting screen time for students aged 13 to 17. They apply the School Student occupation filter, observe the age group bar chart and purposeless usage doughnut, and note that habitual scrolling is prevalent even in younger age bands, supporting intervention proposals.
Filter: Occupation = School Student
Use Case 5
Relationship Status and Social Comparison
A clinical psychologist wants to explore whether single individuals report higher social comparison scores than those in relationships. They cycle through the Relationship filter options while keeping the Social Comparison metric active on the trend line, comparing how usage-to-comparison curves differ by relationship status.
Filter: Relationship = Single vs. In a relationship
Use Case 6
Platform Adoption Benchmarking
A digital marketing strategist wants to understand platform reach across different demographic segments. They cycle through occupation and gender filters to observe how the platform adoption bars and the top platform KPI shift across segments, informing targeted campaign decisions for YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok.
Filter: Gender + Occupation combinations
